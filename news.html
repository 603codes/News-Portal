Kathmandu Launches Electric Bus Fleet to Combat Air Pollution


By Ramesh Adhikari, Staff Reporter
In a significant move towards sustainable urban transport, Kathmandu Metropolitan City unveiled its first fleet of electric buses on Sunday, aiming to reduce air pollution and modernize public transportation.
The introduction of these electric buses marks a pivotal step in Kathmandu's efforts to address escalating air quality concerns and traffic congestion. The initiative aligns with the city's broader environmental goals and commitment to green infrastructure. The new fleet comprises 40 electric buses, each equipped with zero-emission technology and designed for the city's challenging terrain. The buses will operate on major routes, including Ratnapark to Kalanki and Koteshwor to Sundhara, providing residents with a cleaner and more efficient mode of transportation.
Mayor Bidya Sundar Shakya, during the inauguration ceremony at Tundikhel, stated, "This initiative is a milestone in our journey towards a greener Kathmandu. By adopting electric buses, we aim to set an example for sustainable urban mobility." The project, a collaboration between the Kathmandu Metropolitan City Office and the Ministry of Physical Infrastructure and Transport, received funding support from the Asian Development Bank. The buses were manufactured by a joint venture between Nepal's Eco Vehicle Industries and China's GreenTech Motors.
Local commuters have expressed optimism about the new service. Sita Ram, a daily commuter, shared, "The electric buses are quieter and more comfortable. It's a refreshing change from the old diesel buses."
Environmental experts have lauded the move, highlighting its potential impact on reducing the city's carbon footprint. Dr. Anjali Thapa, an environmental scientist at Tribhuvan University, noted, "Transitioning to electric public transport is crucial for improving air quality and public health in Kathmandu."
The electric bus initiative represents Kathmandu's commitment to sustainable development and environmental stewardship. City officials plan to expand the fleet to 100 buses within the next year, further enhancing the capital's public transportation network.


Nepal's National Cricket Team Prepares for ICC World Cup Qualifiers


By Pratik Sharma, Sports Correspondent
The Nepal national cricket team is gearing up for the ICC World Cup Qualifiers, set to take place in Zimbabwe next month. The team, led by captain Sandeep Lamichhane, has been training intensively at the Tribhuvan University Cricket Ground in Kirtipur.
Nepal secured its spot in the qualifiers after a stellar performance in the ICC Cricket World Cup League 2, finishing among the top teams in the tournament. The squad includes experienced players like Gyanendra Malla and Dipendra Singh Airee, alongside promising young talents such as Kushal Malla and Aasif Sheikh.
Coach Jagat Tamata expressed confidence in the team's preparation, stating, "We have worked hard to build a balanced side. Our focus is on executing our game plan effectively."
The qualifiers will feature 10 teams competing for two spots in the ICC Cricket World Cup 2023. Nepal's first match is scheduled against Scotland on June 18.
Fans are optimistic about Nepal's chances, with many hoping for a historic qualification for the World Cup. "This is a golden opportunity for Nepali cricket," said avid fan Ramesh Gurung.
The team will depart for Zimbabwe on June 10, where they will play a series of warm-up matches before the qualifiers begin.
